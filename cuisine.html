1. Dal Makhni

Dal makhani is a rich, buttery lentil dish originating from the Punjab region of India. It's made with black lentils (urad dal) and kidney beans (rajma), slow-cooked with butter, cream, and various spices. The dish is known for its creamy texture and is a popular vegetarian staple in Indian cuisine.

# Dal Makhni– Delhi (Tibetan colonies, street vendors)
Try at: Majnu Ka Tila (Delhi) or Dolma Aunty’s Makhni Steamed dumplings with spicy red chutney.


2.Chhole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpeas (chole) and deep-fried bread (bhature). It's a delicious and filling meal, often enjoyed for breakfast or lunch, and can be found in various restaurants and street food stalls throughout India.

# Chole Bhature – Delhi & Amritsar
Try at: Sita Ram Diwan Chand (Delhi), Kesar Da Dhaba (Amritsar) A perfect breakfast or brunch. Fluffy bhature and spicy chole with tangy chutney.


3.Butter Paneer

Paneer Butter Masala, also known as Butter Paneer, is a rich and creamy Indian curry made with paneer (Indian cheese), a buttery tomato-based sauce, and a blend of spices. It's a popular dish in Indian restaurants and is often served with roti or naan bread.

# Butter Paneer – Amritsar & Dhaba stops on highways
Try at: Kulcha Land (Amritsar) or roadside dhabas in Punjab/Haryana Served with butter, curd, and spicy pickle.


4.Kachori Sabji

Kachori sabzi is a popular breakfast dish in Varanasi, India says Swad-e-Banaras. It typically consists of kachoris, deep-fried bread stuffed with various fillings, served with a sabzi (vegetable curry). The sabzi commonly used is a pumpkin and potato curry, but can vary depending on regional preferences.

# Kachori Sabzi – Varanasi & Jaipur
Try at: Ram Bhandar (Varanasi), Rawat Kachori (Jaipur) Crispy, spicy, and a classic local breakfast.


5.Rajma Chaval

Rajma Chawal is a North Indian dish consisting of red kidney beans (Rajma) cooked in a flavorful tomato and onion gravy, served with steaming rice (Chawal). It's a hearty, nutritious meal often enjoyed as a staple in Punjabi households and across North India.

# Rajma Chaval – Delhi (especially Old Delhi) & Ludhiana
Try at: Moti Mahal (Delhi – where it was invented) Rich and creamy, perfect with naan.
