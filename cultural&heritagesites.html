The Taj Mahal is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal, and is considered a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. The Red Fort, or Lāl Qilā, is a historic fort built by Shah Jahan in Delhi, India, and served as the seat of the Mughal Empire for over 200 years. The Qutb Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, India, and is a minaret and victory tower built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak. The Ajanta and Ellora caves are rock-cut cave temples in Maharashtra, India, showcasing ancient Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain art and architecture. The Sun Temple, or Konark Sun Temple, is a Hindu temple in Odisha, India, dedicated to the Sun God, known for its impressive architecture and intricate carvings.


Red Fort: Built with red sandstone, the Red Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi. It served as the main residence and administrative center of the Mughal emperors.


Qutb Minar: This minaret is part of the Qutb complex in Delhi, which also includes the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and the Iron Pillar. The tower was built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak and is a symbol of the Delhi Sultanate.


Ajanta and Ellora Caves: These caves, located in Maharashtra, are renowned for their Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain rock-cut carvings, sculptures, and paintings. The Ajanta caves are particularly known for their Buddhist paintings and the Ellora caves for their monolithic Kailash Temple.


Sun Temple: Located in Odisha, India, this temple is a fine example of Hindu architecture. It's dedicated to the Sun God and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
